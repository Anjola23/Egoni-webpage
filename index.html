Empowering Agriculture Through Data.


Nigeria's agricultural sector holds immense potential, contributing 22.35% of GDP and employing 70% of the workforce (34.5 million farmers). Yet, this potential remains largely untapped due to critical challenges.

Low Yields

Nigerian farmers achieve harvests significantly below regional averages. Maize yields avg 2MT per hectare, compared to 3.8MT in Ethiopia and 6MT in SA.

Limited Access to Finance

Only 2% of farmers have access to credit, hindering investment in improved techniques and inputs.

PriceVolatility

Unpredictable market fluctuations make it difficult for farmers to plan and secure stable income.

Lack of Transparency

Limited data availability creates an opaque system, hindering informed decision-making by investors, policymakers, and farmers themselves.

What we do

USSD-based data collection

We leverage USSD technology to reach even the most remote farmers, regardless of internet access. Farmers can easily register and submit data points using their basic mobile phones.

Comprehensive data

We collect crucial information on land ownership, yields, challenges, and more, offering a holistic picture of the agricultural landscape.

Anonymized and aggregated

Data is anonymized and aggregated to protect individual privacy while providing valuable insights for stakeholders.

Verified and accessible

Our robust data cleaning and verification processes ensure data accuracy, accessible to farmers, governments, NGOs, and businesses through various subscription models and partnerships.
